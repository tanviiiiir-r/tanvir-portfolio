I’m Md Tanvir Rana, an ICT bachelor’s student at JAMK University of Applied Sciences in Finland, where I focus on cybersecurity with a strong interest in SOC operations, ethical hacking, and the use of artificial intelligence in modern defense systems. My work sits at the intersection of practical engineering and academic exploration. I’ve built systems that detect real attacks — from simulating reverse shells in a Wazuh-based SIEM lab to scanning for vulnerabilities in misconfigured Azure environments and building encrypted messaging tools inspired by real-world threat models.

I’ve ranked among the top 5 cybersecurity learners in Finland on LetsDefend, a reflection of my commitment to hands-on growth and operational mastery. I enjoy working on problems that matter — how to make threat detection more intelligent, how to stay ahead of post-quantum risks, and how to build systems that actually hold up when attacked.

Outside of cybersecurity, I mentor exchange students who arrive in Finland from around the world, helping them adjust to both student life and tech environments. I also love visual storytelling, whether through video editing or photography, and I find it helps me think more creatively about complex systems. I’m now preparing for master’s programs that blend cybersecurity, AI, and cloud technologies, with the long-term vision of building security solutions that are intelligent, ethical, and resilient enough for the challenges of tomorrow.
